Notes on socio-demographic variables

  • Risk factors refer to social determinants of health (socio-demographic variables) from the Census of the Population (with the exception of household food insecurity which comes from the CCHS).
  • Region refers to either Ontario or one of the Local Health Integration Networks (LHINs) in Ontario. The Ontario estimates exclude cases with unknown residence (LHINs). Estimates for Local Health Integration Networks (LHINs) were analyzed using survey weights that were calibrated to the LHIN geographic boundaries, where available.
  • Year refers to the calendar year in which the survey was conducted.
  • Percentage is a prevalence estimate referring to the percentage of the population with the risk factor aged 15+.
  • Standard error is a measure of how much the estimates vary. Typically, rates calculated using fewer case counts have higher standard errors. (Available for household food insecurity only.)
  • Upper and Lower 95% CI (confidence interval) are estimates of the potential lower and upper value of the age-standardized incidence/mortality rate. (Available for household food insecurity only.)
  • Coefficient of Variance (CV) is a measure to determine the sampling variability of the estimate, calculated by dividing standard deviation by mean for each estimate and multiplying by 100. CV was calculated for household food insecurity only.
  • CV flag refers to the estimated population corresponding to the region, sex, age and year selected.
  • Boot strapping techniques were used to obtain variance estimates and 95% confidence intervals of all estimates.

Notes on chronic disease risk factors

  • Risk factors refer to social determinants of health (socio-demographic variables) come from the Census of the Population (with the exception of household food insecurity which comes from the CCHS).
  • Region refers to either Ontario or one of the Local Health Integration Networks (LHINs) in Ontario. The Ontario estimates exclude cases with unknown residence (LHINs).
  • Sex refers to a person's sex as recorded on health records, rather than their gender identity.
  • Age group refers to the age at diagnosis for the cancer and are categorized differently for different cancer sites.
  • Years refer to the calendar year in which a cancer was diagnosed.
  • Age standardized rate refers to the mean for age standardized incidence/mortality rate per 100,000 person-years, age-adjusted to the 2011 Canadian population; 95% Confidence intervals (CI), Tiwari method, for rates.
  • Standard error (for age-standardized incidence/mortality rate) is a measure of how much the age standardized rates vary. Typically, rates calculated using fewer case counts have higher standard errors.
  • Upper and Lower 95% CI (confidence interval) are an estimate of the potential lower and upper value of the age-standardized incidence/mortality rate.
  • Count refers to the number of incidence/mortality; All case counts are randomly rounded to multiples of 5 in consideration of personal health information and the protection of privacy. As a result, sums of subgroup counts (e.g. LHIN) may not equal total counts (e.g. Ontario).
  • Population refers to the estimated population corresponding to the region, sex, age and year selected.
  • RSE (Relative Standard Error as a percentage) is a measure of how reliable the rates are, calculated by dividing standard error by mean for each age-standardized incidence/mortality rate and multiplying by 100. If the RSE is in excess of 23%, the estimate is considered to be imprecise and the estimate is suppressed. To indicate this, the RSE flag will be set to 1. When the RSE is below 23%, the RSE flag is set to 0.
  • Missing data indicates imprecise estimates, or suppressed data to protect personal health information.
